Sequence: order matters here

How the paper actually gets built

Each step depends on the one before it, so the order below is load-bearing, not just a checklist.

1

Shoug

Write the Introduction

Sets the paper's direction before anything else starts.

2

Layan

Collect 10–15 scholarly sources

IEEE, ACM, Springer, ScienceDirect, Google Scholar.

3

Shoug

Write Literature Review + Analysis

Built directly on the sources Layan gathered in step 2; folds in sustainability considerations alongside the ethical and social analysis.

4

Layan

Write Methodology

Frames the paper as a literature-based review using secondary sources.

5

Shoug

Write Recommendations

Follows from the Analysis findings; each recommendation is checked for practicality and long-term sustainability.

6

Layan

Write Conclusion

7

Layan

Write Abstract

Written last, once the full paper exists to summarize.

8

Shoug

Final merge, edit & consistency pass

Reads the whole draft as one voice; plagiarism check; formatting.

Why this split

Content-weighted, not section-count-weighted. Introduction, Literature Review, Analysis, and Recommendations carry most of the rubric's content marks and the paper's largest section (Analysis), so Shoug's share reflects that, not just a bigger slice for its own sake. Layan's sections (Abstract, Methodology, Conclusion, References) are shorter and more contained, which offsets a heavier workload elsewhere this term.
