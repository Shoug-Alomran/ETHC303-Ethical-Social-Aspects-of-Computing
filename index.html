Project Scope

Key Areas of Analysis

Our research breaks down the societal impact of AI and expert systems — across healthcare, finance, and beyond — into four primary dimensions.

Framework Evaluation

Assessing traditional ethical models (Utilitarian, Deontological) against the dilemmas raised by AI and expert systems in clinical and financial decision-making.

Read more

Data Integrity

Analyzing how bias enters training data and propagates through diagnostic and credit-scoring expert systems, causing structural inequality.

Read more

Societal Impact

Mapping the ripple effects of AI-driven decisions on patients, borrowers, and marginalized communities across healthcare and finance.

Empirical Research

Quantifying Ethical Trade-offs

Our research goes beyond philosophical discussion by implementing empirical fairness metrics across real-world datasets from healthcare, finance, and criminal justice. We analyze the inherent tension between model accuracy and fairness constraints.

  • Evaluation of demographic parity vs. equalized odds.
  • Case study application on healthcare risk-prediction and criminal justice risk assessments.
  • Analysis of feedback loops in credit-scoring and loan-approval expert systems.

Process

Project Methodology

Structured approach from problem identification to solution proposal.

Phase 1

Topic Selection & Framing

Identifying core ethical dilemmas raised by AI and expert systems in healthcare, finance, and other domains, and establishing the research boundaries.

Phase 2

Literature Review

Surveying existing academic literature on AI in healthcare, finance, computer science, philosophy, and legal studies.

Phase 3 (Current)

Framework Development

Building an analytical model to evaluate specific algorithmic architectures against established ethical principles.

Phase 4

Empirical Analysis

Applying our framework to real-world datasets and case studies.

Phase 5

Recommendations

Proposing actionable technical and policy-based solutions.
